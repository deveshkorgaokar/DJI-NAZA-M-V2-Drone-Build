# GPS & Compass Calibration (DJI NAZA-M V2)

This guide explains how to properly calibrate the **GPS + Compass module** for DJI NAZA-M V2.

---

## ✅ Before Calibration (Important)

### GPS/Compass Placement Rules
- Keep the GPS/compass module **away from power wires**
- Keep it away from ESC wires and battery cable
- Mount the GPS arrow facing **forward**
- Avoid mounting near metal screws/nuts if possible

✅ Best place: top plate/mast on frame

---

## ✅ Compass Calibration Steps ("Compass Dance")

### Step 1: Power On
1. Turn ON transmitter (TX)
2. Connect the battery to the drone (power ON NAZA)

Wait for NAZA LED normal blinking.

---

### Step 2: Enter Compass Calibration Mode
Flip your flight mode switch **quickly** between GPS and Failsafe (or Atti) **6–10 times**
until the NAZA LED shows compass calibration mode.

✅ It will usually show **solid yellow** or calibration indication.

---

### Step 3: Horizontal Rotation
Hold the drone level (parallel to the ground)

Rotate it **360° slowly** (one full round)

✅ When done correctly, the LED will change mode.

---

### Step 4: Vertical Rotation
Now hold the drone nose down (vertical)

Rotate it **360° slowly** again

✅ Calibration is successful if the LED returns to normal blinking.

---

## ❌ If Calibration Fails
If the LED shows red blinking or an error:

### Fix Checklist
✅ Move away from:
- steel gates
- vehicles
- metal tables
- power transformers / high voltage lines

✅ Check the GPS cable connection  
✅ Re-mount the GPS away from PDB/ESC wires  
✅ Try again in open ground

---

## ✅ Best Practice
Always do compass calibration:
- in an open outdoor area
- before first GPS flight
- after major repairs or component changes
- after moving to a new area (sometimes helpful)
